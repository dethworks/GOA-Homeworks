Joseph Stalin

Joseph Vissarionovich Stalin[f] (born Dzhugashvili;[g] 18 December [O.S. 6 December] 1878 5 March 
1953)
was a Soviet politician and revolutionary who led the Soviet Union from 1924 until his death in
1953. He
held power as General Secretary of the Communist Party from 1922 to 1952 and as Chairman of the
Council
 of Ministers from 1941 until his death. Initially governing as part of a collective leadership,
Stalin
consolidated power to become a dictator by the 1930s. He codified his Leninist interpretation of
Marxism
as MarxismLeninism, while the totalitarian political system he established became known as
Stalinism.

Family

Born into a poor Georgian family in Gori, Russian Empire, Stalin attended 
the Tiflis Theological Seminary before joining the Marxist Russian Social 
Democratic Labour Party. He raised funds for Vladimir Lenin's Bolshevik 
faction through robberies, kidnappings and protection rackets, and edited 
the party's newspaper, Pravda. Repeatedly arrested, he underwent internal
exiles to Siberia. After the Bolsheviks seized power in the October 
Revolution of 1917
